  <Biography eyebrow="Biography">
    <Bio>
Like many Millennials who grew up with the early web, my story began with foundational programming, design curiosity, and the unforgettable screech of a 56k dial-up modem.

Back in 1995, as a kid exploring system-of-record logic in GW-BASIC and experimenting with the new Windows 95 MS Paint toolbar, I became hooked on how logic, UI, and online connectivity intersected. That fascination with how data travels over a wire and how interfaces are shaped set me on a journey to develop internet-based software, which continues to shape how I design, develop, and scale modern SaaS solutions today.

Navigating the professional landscape early on required deep resilience and adaptability. A lack of opportunities in the local Pakistani market during 2001 to 2003 pushed me to look globally, opening the door to international freelancing through GetACoder in 2004 and, later, [GetAFreelancer](https://www.my.freelancer.com/u/junaidart) between 2006 to 2011. This period became a masterclass in versatility, as the nature of my projects quickly evolved from small site fixes, retainers to architecting major SaaS-based platforms such as [QuickBrowse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quickbrowse), [cometocapetown](https://www.cometocapetown.com), and [crea8ivedesign](https://crea8ivedesign.junaid.guru).

I moved to the UK in 2011, where I stepped into Senior and Lead engineering roles at companies such as iPER Media, [SchoolStickers](https://www.schoolstickers.com), [Inspired Thinking Group](https://inspiredthinking.group), and PushStudios. Driven by an entrepreneurial mindset, I partnered with a small agency in partnership with [StudioA2](https://studioa2.co.uk), focusing on white-label development services for major UK design agencies, and scaled the engineering team to 8 developers.

My path eventually transitioned from writing lines of code to architecting complex enterprise systems and scaling engineering organisations. I joined Connect Group as a Lead Developer, which was later acquired by Inspired Thinking Group, and I was promoted to Solutions Architect at ITG ([Storyteq](https://storyteq.com)). Over the past decade, I have focused on scaling small engineering groups into high-performing, mid-sized teams. This experience completely reframed how I view technical leadership. I realised that success isn't measured by the volume of code we merge, but by how effectively we empower the teams around us. True delivery means ensuring that the engineering teams relying on our platforms can move faster, stay stable, and build with absolute confidence.

This perspective marks a significant maturation from my early career. Ten years ago, I used to describe myself as "18/24"—always on, always available, and treating constant personal involvement as a badge of honour. Today, my priority has shifted to building independent, high-performing teams and resilient system architectures that operate seamlessly without my daily hands-on intervention. What started as a childhood wonder at a flickering monitor has evolved into a career dedicated to architecting robust enterprise solutions, with stronger uptime and scalable infrastructure.

    </Bio>

      <Goals>
        <li>Make the right architecture the default: guardrails in the platform, not rules in a doc.</li>
        <li>Treat developer velocity as a business metric, measured built-to-released rather than spec-to-built.</li>
        <li>Distribute leadership by design. Build the pipeline instead of keeping a list of names I sponsor.</li>
